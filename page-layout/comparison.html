Healthcare System Comparison: U.S. vs. U.K.

This page compares the healthcare systems of the United States and United Kingdom, focusing on their approaches to value-based care implementation.

Structural Differences

The U.S. operates a predominantly private, market-driven system, while the U.K.'s National Health Service provides universal, publicly-funded coverage.

Cost and Efficiency Metrics

Despite different funding models, both systems face similar challenges in cost containment and quality improvement.
